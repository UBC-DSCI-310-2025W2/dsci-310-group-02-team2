# Purpose

This Code of Conduct outlines the standards of behavior for members of DSCI 310 Group 2 while working on our course project. Our goal is to create a collaborative, respectful, and productive environment where all team members can contribute effectively. This code supports our shared objective of producing a high-quality, reproducible data science project while developing our skills in data analysis, version control, and project collaboration.

This Code of Conduct applies to all project-related activities, including:
- Team meetings
- GitHub collaboration
- Group chats and messaging
- Work on shared project files
- Communication about the project

# Our Values
- *Respect*: All team members should treat each other with professionalism and respect. Everyone’s ideas, questions, and perspectives are valued.
- *Collaboration*: We actively include each other in discussions and decisions. Team members should support each other, especially when someone is stuck or facing challenges.
- *Accountability*: Each member is responsible for completing their assigned tasks and communicating progress honestly.
- *Transparency*: Work, decisions, and issues should be communicated openly to maintain trust and efficiency.
- *Reproducibility and Integrity*: As a data science team, we commit to following reproducible and trustworthy workflows, including proper version control and documentation.

# Expected Behavior

Team members agree to:

### Communication
- Respond to team messages within 24 hours whenever possible.

- Communicate concerns, delays, or challenges early.

- Participate actively in group discussions and decision making.

### Meetings

- Attend the weekly team meeting (40–60 minutes).

- Inform the team in advance if unable to attend.

- Review meeting notes if a meeting is missed.

### Participation

- Contribute 6–8 hours of work per milestone outside meetings.

- Participate in discussions and decision making.

- Support teammates when they need help.

### Work Quality

- Complete assigned tasks by agreed deadlines.

- Follow the project workflow using GitHub Flow.

- Maintain organized, reproducible, and documented work.

### Collaboration

- Be open to feedback and constructive criticism.

- Respect different working styles and skill levels.

- Ask for help when needed and offer help when possible.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- The following behaviors are not acceptable within the team:

- Ignoring communication or repeatedly failing to respond

- Consistently missing meetings without notice
- Failing to complete agreed tasks without informing the team

- Dismissing or ignoring teammates’ ideas

- Hostile, disrespectful, or unprofessional communication

- Any form of discrimination, harassment, or bullying

### Reporting Issues or Concerns

If a team member experiences issues affecting collaboration, they should:

1. Start with a private conversation with the relevant teammate to clarify the issue.

2. If the issue persists, raise it with the full team to discuss possible solutions.

3. If a resolution cannot be reached, contact a member of the course teaching team.

Team members should raise concerns early so problems can be addressed before deadlines are affected.

### Conflict Resolution and Consequences

If a team member repeatedly fails to meet expectations:

1. The team will discuss the issue privately with the individual.

2. If the problem continues, the entire team will discuss possible adjustments (e.g., redistributing work).

3. If issues remain unresolved, the team will notify the course instructors or teaching assistants.

The goal of these steps is to resolve problems constructively and maintain a healthy team environment.

### Work Distribution

1. Tasks will be assigned at the beginning of each milestone.

2. Roles will be based on experience, interest, or rotation if preferences are similar.

3. Each team member is expected to contribute a similar amount of work overall.

### Meetings and Documentation

- One weekly meeting will be scheduled using When2Meet.

- Additional meetings may occur before deadlines if necessary.

- Each meeting will have a designated note-taker.

- Notes will be shared with the team to ensure transparency.

### Commitment

By participating in this project, team members agree to follow this Code of Conduct and contribute to a collaborative and respectful working environment.
